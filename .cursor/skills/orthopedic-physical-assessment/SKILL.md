# Orthopedic Physical Assessment — Clinical Reference

Source: *Orthopedic Physical Assessment* by Magee & Manske (7th ed., Elsevier, 2021)

---

## Chapter 1: Principles and Concepts

### Total Musculoskeletal Assessment Sequence

1. **Patient history** — subjective; symptom behavior; mechanism of injury
2. **Observation** — posture, asymmetry, deformity, skin, edema, overt pain behavior
3. **Examination of movement** — active → passive → resisted isometric
4. **Special (diagnostic) tests** — confirm/refute hypothesis
5. **Reflexes and cutaneous distribution** — dermatomes, myotomes, reflexes
6. **Joint play movements** — arthrokinematics; accessory motion
7. **Palpation** — bony landmarks, soft tissue, tenderness
8. **Diagnostic imaging** — X-ray, MRI, CT, ultrasound; ordered by finding

### Key History Questions (20-Point Checklist)

| # | Question | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Age and sex | Age-related pathology prevalence (e.g., OA > 40, growth disorders in teens) |
| 2 | Occupation/activity | Demands, posture, repetitive stress |
| 3 | Chief complaint / why now | Functional limitation; patient goals |
| 4 | Mechanism of injury (macro/micro trauma) | Direction/magnitude of force → which structures likely injured |
| 5 | Onset (sudden vs. insidious) | Acute injury vs. overuse; is there a specific episode? |
| 6 | Location of symptoms now | Local vs. referred; specific vs. diffuse |
| 7 | Character and behavior of pain | Severity, irritability, quality, spread |
| 8 | Aggravating/easing factors | Mechanical vs. systemic; irritability |
| 9 | Duration and frequency | Acute (<7 days), subacute (7–49 days), chronic (>7 weeks) |
| 10 | Previous episodes | History of same injury; recurrence pattern |
| 11 | Kinetic chain involvement | Foot → knee → hip → spine relationships |
| 12 | Trend of symptoms | Worsening, improving, static |
| 13–20 | Additional: medications, sleep, bilateral symptoms, autonomic symptoms, prior treatment, psychosocial factors | Completeness of differential diagnosis |

**Pain Severity for Repetitive Stress (7-Level Scale):**
- Level 1: Pain after activity | Level 4: Pain during activity affecting performance | Level 7: Pain disturbing sleep

### Red Flags — Refer to Physician

| System | Red Flag Signs |
|---|---|
| Cancer | Persistent night pain; unexplained weight loss >5% in 4 weeks; unusual lumps; sores not healing |
| Cardiovascular | Chest pain/heaviness; pulsating pain; severe calf pain without injury; discolored feet; unexplained swelling |
| Neurological | Bilateral pins and needles; drop attacks; fainting; sudden weakness; progressive gait disturbance; saddle anesthesia |
| GI/GU | Incontinence; severe abdominal pain; altered bowel/bladder function |
| Miscellaneous | Fever, night sweats; swelling with no injury history |

### Yellow Flags — Extends Examination / Cautions

- Abnormal symptom patterns; bilateral symptoms; symptoms peripheralizing
- Multiple nerve root involvement; saddle anesthesia; UMN signs
- Psychosocial stressors; fear-avoidance beliefs (FABQ > 34 for work scale)
- Progressive weakness; multiple inflamed joints

### Pain Dimensions

Pain is multidimensional (Sensory, Affective, Behavioral, Cognitive, Sociocultural-Ethnocultural). Always consider all domains.

**Differentiation: Musculoskeletal vs. Systemic Pain:**
| Musculoskeletal | Systemic |
|---|---|
| Aggravated by mechanical stress | Not aggravated by movement |
| Sharp or superficial ache | Deep aching or throbbing |
| Usually intermittent | Constant or waves of spasm |
| Lessens at night | Disturbs sleep |

## Examination Principles

### Sequence of Movement Testing

1. **Active (AROM)** first — patient-generated; reveals joint range, motor control, willingness to move; tests contractile + inert + nervous tissue together
2. **Passive (PROM)** second — examiner-controlled; tests inert + nervous tissue; apply overpressure to assess end feel
3. **Resisted isometric (RISM)** last — patient contracts maximally while examiner prevents movement; tests contractile tissue in resting/neutral position

**Key examination rules:**
- Test uninvolved side first (establishes normal baseline)
- Do painful movements last (avoid symptom overflow)
- Sustain or repeat movements if history indicates cumulative symptoms
- Resisted isometric done with joint in **resting/neutral position** to minimize inert tissue stress
- Myotome testing: hold contraction ≥5 seconds to reveal weakness

## End Feel Classification (Cyriax)

### Normal End Feels

| End Feel | Sensation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Bone-to-bone** | Hard, unyielding, painless | Elbow extension |
| **Soft-tissue approximation** | Yielding, mushy compression | Knee/elbow flexion |
| **Tissue stretch** | Firm, springy, elastic resistance | Ankle DF, shoulder ER, finger extension |

### Abnormal End Feels

| End Feel | Characteristics | Associated Pathology |
|---|---|---|
| **Early muscle spasm** | Sudden hard arrest early in ROM | Acute inflammation |
| **Late muscle spasm** | Sudden arrest near end range | Instability, irritability |
| **Mushy tissue stretch** | Like tissue stretch but less elastic | Tight/shortened muscle |
| **Spasticity** | Velocity-dependent resistance | Upper motor neuron lesion |
| **Hard capsular** | Firm, thick stretching quality before expected end range | Frozen shoulder (chronic capsulitis) |
| **Soft capsular (boggy)** | Similar to tissue stretch, restricted ROM; resistance through range | Acute synovitis, edema, hemarthrosis |
| **Abnormal bone-to-bone** | Hard but before expected end range | Osteophyte formation |
| **Empty** | Severe pain stops movement; no mechanical resistance; no spasm | Acute subacromial bursitis, tumor |
| **Springy block** | Rebound effect; thick stretching feel | Meniscus tear (locked knee) |

> **Clinical implication:** If pain predominates → gentler assessment/treatment; if restriction predominates → more vigorous treatment appropriate.

### Capsular Patterns (Common Joints)

| Joint | Pattern (Most → Least Restricted) |
|---|---|
| Glenohumeral | Lateral rotation, abduction, medial rotation |
| Cervical spine | Side flexion = rotation, extension |
| Lumbar spine | Side flexion = rotation, extension |
| Hip | Flexion, abduction, medial rotation |
| Knee | Flexion, extension |
| Talocrural | Plantar flexion, dorsiflexion |
| Elbow | Flexion, extension |
| Wrist | Flexion = extension |
| First MTP | Extension, flexion |

**Noncapsular patterns:** Local ligament adhesion; internal derangement (e.g., meniscal block of extension); extra-articular lesions; constant-length phenomenon (neural).

## Contractile vs. Inert Tissue Differential Diagnosis

### Cyriax's Tissue Classification

**Contractile tissue:** Muscle, tendon, enthesis (muscle–bone attachment)
- Pain with active AND resisted isometric movement in same direction
- Passive movement may be pain-free in that direction

**Inert tissue:** Joint capsule, ligament, bursa, cartilage, dura mater, blood vessel
- Pain with passive movement only
- Resisted isometric usually painless (unless compression occurs)

**Patterns of Inert Tissue Lesions:**
1. **Full, pain-free ROM** → no inert lesion tested
2. **Pain + limited ROM in every direction** → capsular pattern (arthritis, capsulitis)
3. **Pain + limited/excessive ROM in some directions** → noncapsular pattern (ligament sprain, internal derangement)
4. **Limited, pain-free ROM** → asymptomatic restriction (e.g., osteophyte)

### Strain/Sprain Grading

| Feature | 1° Strain | 2° Strain | 3° Strain | 1° Sprain | 2° Sprain | 3° Sprain |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fibers torn | Few | ~50% | All | Few | ~50% | All |
| Weakness | Minor | Moderate | Moderate (reflex inhibition) | None | Minor–moderate | Minor–moderate |
| Instability | No | No | No | No | Moderate | Moderate–major |
| Pain on isometric | Yes | Yes | No | No | No | No |
| Pain on stretch | Yes | Yes | No | Yes | Yes | No |

## Neurological Assessment

### Dermatomes, Myotomes, and Reflexes

**Cervical:**
| Level | Dermatome | Key Muscles (Myotome) | Reflex |
|---|---|---|---|
| C4 | Top of shoulder | Diaphragm, shoulder shrugging | — |
| C5 | Lateral arm | Deltoid, biceps | Biceps |
| C6 | Thumb, index finger | Wrist extensors, biceps | Brachioradialis |
| C7 | Middle finger | Triceps, wrist flexors | Triceps |
| C8 | Ring/little finger | Finger flexors, hand intrinsics | — |
| T1 | Medial forearm | Hand intrinsics | — |

**Lumbar:**
| Level | Dermatome | Key Muscles | Reflex |
|---|---|---|---|
| L2 | Anterior thigh | Hip flexors | — |
| L3 | Medial knee | Quadriceps | Knee jerk (partial) |
| L4 | Medial leg/foot | Tibialis anterior | Patellar |
| L5 | Dorsum foot, big toe | Extensor hallucis longus, gluteus medius | — |
| S1 | Lateral/plantar foot | Peronei, gastrocnemius | Achilles |
| S2 | Posterior thigh | Hamstrings | — |

**Reflex grading:** 0 = absent; 1 = hyporeflexia; 2 = normal; 3 = hyperreflexia; 4 = clonus

**Key differentials:**
- **Radiculopathy** (nerve root): radicular pain + dermatomal sensory + myotomal weakness + reflex change
- **Peripheral nerve lesion**: follows peripheral nerve distribution (not dermatomal)
- **Myelopathy**: upper motor neuron signs; bilateral; bowel/bladder involvement
- **Plexopathy**: brachial or lumbosacral plexus; multiple root levels involved

### Nerve Injury Classification (Seddon)

| Grade | Lesion | Motor/Sensory | Recovery |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Neuropraxia** | Conduction block; no Wallerian degeneration | Weakness + numbness; proprioception affected | Minutes to days |
| **Axonotmesis** | Axon disrupted; endoneurium intact | Complete motor + sensory + autonomic loss | Months (1 mm/day) |
| **Neurotmesis** | Complete disruption | Complete loss; anesthesia | Requires surgery; incomplete |

**Double-crush syndrome:** Compression at two points along a nerve (e.g., cervical root + carpal tunnel) → cumulative effect causing symptoms at distal site.

### Neural Tension Tests

- **SLR (Sciatic nerve):** Positive if reproduces symptoms; sensitize with ankle DF or neck flexion
- **Slump test:** Seated slump → neck flexion → knee extension → ankle DF; mimics sitting (car, desk)
- **ULNT 1–4 (Brachial plexus/median/radial/ulnar):** Reproduce upper limb symptoms
- **Prone knee bend:** Femoral nerve (L3); stretches anterior nerve root

> Tension tests positive ONLY if: (1) reproduce patient's symptoms, (2) altered by sensitizing movements at distal body part, OR (3) asymmetric response.

## Joint Instability

| Type | Definition | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Translational (mechanical)** | Loss of arthrokinematic control | Muscles fail to stabilize small joint movements |
| **Anatomic (clinical/gross)** | Excessive physiological movement; subluxation/dislocation imminent | Patient apprehensive at end range |
| **Functional** | Either type during functional movement | High-speed or loaded movements reveal it |

**Circle concept:** Injury causing instability on one side of a joint may concurrently injure structures on the opposite side (e.g., anterior shoulder dislocation → posterior capsule injury).

## Muscle Strength Testing (Modified Oxford Scale)

| Grade | Label | Performance |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Normal | Full ROM vs. gravity + maximal resistance |
| 4 | Good | Full ROM vs. gravity + moderate resistance |
| 3 | Fair | Full ROM vs. gravity only |
| 2 | Poor | Full ROM gravity eliminated |
| 1 | Trace | Contraction palpable; no joint motion |
| 0 | Zero | No contraction |

**Positional/stretch weakness:** Muscle weakness resulting from chronic lengthening or shortening; test in multiple positions to differentiate pathological weakness (all positions) from positional weakness (specific angle).

## Special Tests — Framework

**Use special tests as part of a cluster, not in isolation:**
- Select based on pre-test probability from history + observation
- Positive test confirms hypothesis; negative test alone does not rule out
- Sensitivity (true positive rate) vs. specificity (true negative rate)
- **Highlighted tests in Magee = clinically validated and/or statistically significant contribution to diagnosis**

**Common test categories by joint:**
- **Shoulder:** Impingement (Neer, Hawkins-Kennedy); rotator cuff (Empty Can/Jobe, drop arm, Gerber's lift-off, belly press); instability (apprehension/relocation/surprise, anterior/posterior drawer); SLAP (O'Brien, Speed's); AC joint (Scarf test)
- **Elbow:** Lateral epicondylalgia (Cozen, Mill's); medial epicondylalgia; valgus stress (UCL); cubital tunnel (Tinel's at elbow)
- **Wrist/Hand:** CTS (Phalen's, Tinel's at wrist, median nerve compression); TFCC (DRUJ grind, fovea sign); scaphoid (anatomical snuffbox tenderness, Watson's)
- **Hip:** FAI (FADIR/impingement test); trochanteric bursitis (palpation); labral (FABER, Patrick's); gluteus medius (Trendelenburg); OA (log roll, Patrick's FABER)
- **Knee:** ACL (Lachman's, anterior drawer, pivot shift); PCL (posterior drawer, posterior sag); MCL/LCL (valgus/varus stress at 0° and 30°); meniscus (McMurray's, Thessaly's, Apley's); patellofemoral (Clarke's, J-sign, patellar apprehension)
- **Ankle/Foot:** Lateral ligaments (ATFL — anterior drawer, talar tilt); deltoid; Achilles (Thompson's); Ottawa rules
- **Lumbar spine:** SLR (L4–S1 nerve root); femoral nerve stretch; slump; crossed SLR (central disc); quadrant/extension test
- **Cervical spine:** Spurling's (foraminal compression); distraction; Lhermitte's sign; vertebral artery tests (VBI screening)
- **Sacroiliac:** Sacral compression/distraction; FABER (Patrick's); posterior shear (thigh thrust); Gaenslen's

## Palpation Principles

- Palpate last (after history, observation, movement tests) or at end of examination
- Compare bilaterally (temperature, texture, tenderness, swelling)
- Identify: bony landmarks, muscle bulk/atrophy, tendon consistency, joint lines, warmth, edema
- **Tendon grading:** Paratenonitis (thickening) vs. tendinosis (intratendinous degeneration; palpable nodule possible)
- **Point tenderness:** Localizes to specific structure when patient points to a spot ≤1 cm²
- Always palpate adjacent joints and kinetic chain segments

## Diagnostic Imaging — Selection Guide

| Modality | Best For |
|---|---|
| **X-ray** | Fracture, bone alignment, OA (joint space), calcification, scoliosis |
| **MRI** | Soft tissue (ligament, tendon, cartilage, labrum, disc, nerve); gold standard for many |
| **CT scan** | Complex fractures, bone detail, bone fragments; better than MRI for cortical bone |
| **Ultrasound** | Dynamic tendon assessment; superficial structures; guided injections |
| **Bone scan** | Stress fractures (early), metastases, osteomyelitis |

## Regional Assessment Précis

### Cervical Spine
- History: headache, dizziness, arm paresthesia, bilateral symptoms, swallowing
- VBI screen before cervical manipulation: 5-position test
- AROM: flexion, extension, lateral flexion, rotation
- Neurological: C3–T1 dermatomes/myotomes; cervical myelopathy signs
- Capsular pattern: side flexion = rotation, extension
- Key special tests: Spurling's (radiculopathy), distraction test, Lhermitte's, upper limb neural tension tests

### Thoracic Spine
- Unique: rib cage limits ROM; assess for costochondritis, T4 syndrome
- AROM: flexion, extension, rotation (most available)
- Assess for kyphosis, scoliosis (Adam's forward bend test)
- Capsular pattern: side flexion = rotation, extension

### Lumbar Spine
- History: radiation, bilateral symptoms, bowel/bladder (cauda equina — emergency referral)
- AROM: flexion, extension, lateral flexion, rotation + repeated movements (McKenzie)
- Neurological: L1–S2 dermatomes/myotomes; SLR, crossed SLR, slump
- Capsular pattern: side flexion = rotation, extension
- Key special tests: SLR (L4–S1), femoral nerve stretch (L2–L4), Waddell's signs (5 signs → psychogenic)

### Sacroiliac Joint
- No capsular pattern; pain when joint stressed
- Key tests: Sacral compression/distraction, FABER (Patrick's), thigh thrust, Gaenslen's, active SLR
- Cluster of ≥3 provocation tests increases diagnostic accuracy

### Shoulder (Glenohumeral + AC + SC)
- Capsular pattern: ER > abduction > IR
- Assess scapular kinematics (SICK scapula, winging)
- Instability: apprehension/relocation; check circle concept
- RC tears: drop arm, empty can (supraspinatus); Gerber's lift-off (subscapularis); hornblower's sign (teres minor)
- Impingement: Neer, Hawkins-Kennedy, painful arc (60–120°)
- SLAP: O'Brien, Speed's, Yergason's (biceps)

### Elbow
- Capsular pattern: flexion, extension (equally limited in acute capsulitis)
- Lateral epicondylalgia: Cozen's (resisted wrist extension), Mill's (passive wrist flexion + elbow extension), chair test
- UCL: valgus stress at 30° flexion; moving valgus stress test
- Ulnar nerve: cubital tunnel (Tinel's, elbow flexion test)

### Wrist and Hand
- CTS: Phalen's (60-sec wrist flexion), Tinel's, carpal compression test
- Scaphoid: anatomical snuffbox tenderness + scaphoid shift test (Watson)
- TFCC: DRUJ instability, fovea sign, piano-key test
- Trigger finger: palpate A1 pulley; assess triggering phenomenon
- Functional grip patterns: power grip, precision pinch, key (lateral) pinch

### Hip
- Capsular pattern: flexion > abduction > IR (variable)
- FAI assessment: FADIR (flexion-adduction-IR) — pain = impingement
- Labral tear: FABER with overpressure; click/clunk at end range
- Trochanteric bursitis: palpation over greater trochanter; pain with FABER, IR
- Trendelenburg sign: gluteus medius weakness; pelvis drops on unsupported side during single-leg stance

### Knee
- Capsular pattern: flexion > extension
- ACL: Lachman's (most sensitive, 90%), anterior drawer, pivot shift; assess in 20–30° flexion (Lachman's)
- Meniscus: McMurray's (rotation + varus/valgus at various flexion angles); Thessaly's (standing single-leg rotation at 20°); Apley's
- Patellofemoral: Clarke's (patellar grind), J-sign, lateral patellar tilt, patellar apprehension
- PCL: posterior sag, posterior drawer at 90°, dial test (tibial ER at 30° and 90°)

### Ankle and Foot
- Ottawa Rules: X-ray indicated if bone tenderness at posterior malleolus or inability to weight-bear
- ATFL: anterior drawer (ankle plantarflexion + anterior force), talar tilt (inversion stress)
- Achilles: Thompson's test (calf squeeze); assess for tendinopathy vs. rupture
- Plantar fascia: palpate medial calcaneal tuberosity; windlass test

### Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ)
- Capsular pattern: limitation of mouth opening
- Normal opening: 35–50 mm (3 finger breadths)
- Assess: clicking, crepitus, lateral deviation; rule out cervical contribution
- Bruxism, malocclusion, dental history relevant

## Observation: Key Postural Assessment Points

**Anterior view:** Nose, sternum, umbilicus in line; shoulder height symmetry; pelvic level
**Lateral view:** Ear, acromion, high iliac crest, anterior lateral malleolus should align
**Posterior view:** Spine alignment; scapular position; popliteal crease height

**Neutral pelvis:** ASIS 1–2 finger widths below PSIS; assess whether patient can (1) achieve, (2) hold during distal movement, (3) control dynamically (walking, running).

**Overt pain behaviors (observe without alerting patient):**
- Guarding, bracing, rubbing, grimacing, sighing

## Functional Assessment Levels (Reiman & Manske Framework)

| Level | Type | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| I | Subjective report | Self-report questionnaires; FABQ, TSK, SF-36 |
| II | Impairment | MMT, goniometry, sensation, joint play |
| III | Static posture/balance | Plumb line alignment; single-leg stance |
| IV | Dynamic posture/movement patterns | Gait, transfer movements, functional reach |
| VII | Physical performance (single plane) | Single-leg squat, step-down, lunge |
| VIII–IX | Performance (multi-plane/explosive) | Hop tests, agility drills, shuttle run |
| XI | Task replication | Sport/occupation simulation, FCE |
